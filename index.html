About Me

My Name is Austin Martin and I am a sophomore at The University of Pittsburgh. I am majoring in information sciences and minoring in computer science. My specialization is in networks and security.

In computer science, I have experience in many languages. These include Java, C/C++, Python, HTML and HTML frameworks. Information science is where my love for technology has blossomed. In IS, I have experience with database management (MySQL), telecommunications, and networks/network security. I have worked with Kali Linux, Red Hat Linux, and Wireshark.

Outside of my studies, I enjoy independently learning topics and skills in tech. Currently I am researching how to defend against "jam, intercept, and replay attacks" in remote keyless entry systems. To do this I am using a Raspberry Pi. I am also interested in real-time computer vision and recognition (OpenCV).

As I am a full time college student, I am also a member of the Three Rivers Battalion Army ROTC and the Pennsylvania Army National Guard. In the National Guard, I shadow an Army Signal Officer. Here I learn how to manage telecommunications for a battalion size element (600 soldiers).
